Tomorrow Water. Today in LA

The Tomorrow Water Process begins with Today in LA. Innovation beyond waste must begin today for a cleaner world tomorrow. LA is targeted as an ideal “water valley” for its necessity, resources, and geography. The necessity to begin this process in LA includes the drastic global climate change that has led to a severe drought in the area. Currently, Executive Order #5 is in place, which aims for a 20% reduction in water consumption by 2017 and a 50% reduction in import of water by 2020. In 2015, Mayor Garcetti emphasized to the citizens that, “Our relationship with water must evolve. We cannot afford the water policies of the past. We must conserve, recycle, and rethink how we use our water to save money and make sure that we have enough water to keep L.A. growing.” LA also has access to many resources, such as intellectual resources in the forms of research centers and engineering/consulting firms, funding resources from acts such as CA Prop 1 and the Greenhouse Gas Reduction Initiative, and infrastructure resources based on its large metropolitan area containing many existing wastewater treatment plants. Finally, LA’s geography makes it a favorable choice for this project because of its ideal location for development and optimization of solutions for developing nations with similar warm weather climates. LA serves as a promising future “water valley,” owing to its potential to create a center global water industry on LA hub, revitalize LA commerce, and create local jobs, all the while promoting a sustainable and water-friendly community.

Tomorrow Water is a brand of BKT United Inc., a solution provider for water and energy. Our strength lies in fully utilizing the diverse technology portfolio developed on our own and with our partners to deliver comprehensive consulting, engineering, operating, and financing services in designing customized solutions for each client.
